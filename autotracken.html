About the project

AutoTrack is a tool built for freelancers that solves a very specific problem: knowing how long it takes to get paid, how much you have earned this quarter, and what your average rate per client actually is.

The design is built around three clear ideas: giving freelancers a clear picture of their earnings without manual calculations, making sure no invoice gets lost along the way, and helping them make data-driven decisions about who they work with.

What I learned

  • JPA entity modelling with 1:N relationships and mapping between Java conventions and MySQL
  • Writing JPQL queries with named parameters, relationship navigation and aggregate functions such as AVG
  • Separation of concerns in a layered architecture: model, repository, service and util
  • Financial calculations using BigDecimal to guarantee precision in amounts, VAT and income tax withholding
  • Database connection management via the Singleton pattern in HibernateUtil
  • Protecting credentials with environment variables and .gitignore before publishing to GitHub
